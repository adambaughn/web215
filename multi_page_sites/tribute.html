Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.

Still making the world a better place for all of us

Here is more information about Martin Luther King Jr.

  • 1929 - Born on January 15 in Atlanta, Georgia

  • 1948 - Graduated from Morehouse College with a Bachelor of Arts in sociology

  • 1953 - Married Coretta Scott on June 18th.

  • 1955 - Graduated from Boston University with a Ph.D. in systematic theology

  • 1955 - King became recognized nationally for his role in the Montgomery bus boycott.

  • 1957 - King cofounded the Southern Christian Leadership Conference to organize black churches to protest for civil rights reform.

  • 1963 - King helped organize a march in Washington, DC that was attended by more than a quarter million people. King delivered his famous speech known as "I Have a Dream" that day.

  • 1967 - King delivered a speech opposing the war in Vietnam titled "Beyond Vietnam: A Time to Break Silence".

  • 1968 - King organized the "Poor People's Campaign" to bring attention to economic inequality.

  • 1968 - On March 29th, King was assassinated bringing an end to his fight for equality but his memory lives on and his efforts have made the world a better place for all of us.
